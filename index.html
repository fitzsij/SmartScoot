SmaScooSh

Smart Scooter Sharing

The basic pitch

People, who are partially abled
Have places they want to go to
Can use technology to link everything together
We want to bring want to make city areas which are too far to walk and too short to drive accessable, convenient, and easy for people with partial walking abilities using the technology and community
  • Collect lots of data mapping which areas are accessible
  • Fine grain stitching together transport and social systems and infrastructure such as the rail system, casserole club/meals-on-wheels, giant roomba street sweeping machines
  • Engage community & new models of ownership - community driven open source, platform cooperative

Problem Identification

Large groups (elderly, disabled, injured, fatigued, encumbered) of people have partial mobility but find it difficult or impossible to access and get around many places.

Mary

Elderly, going shopping and visiting friends across the city is exhausting

Brian

Likes to socialise but has an injury that makes navigating narrow cobbled streets hazardous

Charlie

Autistic, overstimulation and changes in routine are frightening, getting to his service provider is expensive and time consuming

We map their journeys - from their house to train station, station to shopping mall, shopping mall to cinema, and back; and their schedules - trips they make every morning, evening, week etc

Cost effectiveness/ Business model

Scooter: approx Euro 2000-5000 + business running costs (paying developers, truck to go around city, renting stations)

User payment: annual membership fee + pay per trip or per day

Sponsorship and government funding + partner cross investments

Realisation

Pathway/milestones
